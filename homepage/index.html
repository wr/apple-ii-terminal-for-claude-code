💾 My Rig

Behold: an Apple IIgs in gorgeous platinum, purring along at a blistering 2.8 MHz. But this is no museum piece, friend. This machine is modernized:

  • WiModem 232 Pro on the modem port — it thinks it's dialing a BBS, it's actually doing TCP over my WiFi. The 90s dream, realized.
  • FloppyEmu standing in for the Disk II — a whole shoebox of floppies on one SD card. (Just never, EVER copy files onto the card wrong or you get the dreaded "file not contiguous". Ask me how I know.)
  • The classic AppleColor RGB monitor, still crispy after all these years.

🤖 The AI Project NEW!

OK so here's the crazy part. I hooked the GS up to Claude, an AI from the future. A little bridge program on my Mac reads what I type on the GS, sends it to the AI, and streams the answer back over the modem port. The GS side is pure 65816 assembly running in Super Hi-Res 640 mode — all four glorious colors of it.

Actual screenshot* (*lovingly re-typed because screen capture on a IIgs is a whole thing)

(\_/) (o.o) CLAUDE for the Apple IIgs / > ♥ ------------------------ > how do I get this thing on the internet ● Your WiModem is already doing the hard part. Type ATDS=0 and it dials straight into the bridge -- no acoustic coupler required. ✳ Worked for 4s > _ 

Yes, the AI runs at 9600 baud. Yes, watching it think in real time on 40-year-old hardware absolutely rules. The whole thing (bridge + assembly source) lives on my hard drive next to about 400 unfinished projects.
